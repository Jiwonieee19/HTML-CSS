Position Absolute and Relative

Relative (The Shifty Pawn): A relative piece starts in its normal square but can nudge slightly to the left, right, up, or down. Even if it steps out of its square, all the other pieces still respect that empty square and pretend it is still standing there. Nobody else can steal that spot!

Absolute (The Teleporting Knight): An absolute piece breaks the rules. It completely leaves its square, allowing other pieces to slide right in and take over the space. It can jump and land anywhere it wants, but it has to measure its steps from the edges of the chessboard itself (or the edges of a bigger box it is trapped inside).

Fixed (The Sticker on the Screen): A fixed piece is not even on the board anymore. It is like putting a physical sticker of a King directly onto your computer screen. No matter how much you scroll the chessboard up or down, or how the game changes underneath, that sticker stays frozen in the exact same spot on your glass screen.
